The curator

I scout every home before you see it. Most don't make the list.

I came from fashion. Twenty years of editing collections, walking showrooms, calling the one piece out of a hundred that belonged in the story. You learn to feel it before you can explain it — a cut, a fabric, a room. A place.

I do the same thing with homes now. Walk in, breathe, and check for goosebumps. Not a metaphor — a literal goosebump check at the threshold. No goosebumps, no place. Doesn't matter how the brochure looks.

What I look for is the same thing I used to look for in a perfectly cut coat. Quiet confidence. A thing that arrives already knowing who it is — before you've had a chance to ask.

Nomadikas is what's left after that edit. One percent of what I see. A handful of homes across four continents. Each one chosen because it did something to me, and because it will do something to you.

Counter-positioning

What you won't find at a Nomadikas home.

We built the list by subtraction. If it's easy to find anywhere else, it isn't here. Six things we said no to:

  • ×Resort chains with seven hundred rooms pretending to be boutique.
  • ×Concierge teams you've never spoken to before you arrive.
  • ×Prices that double in peak season without anything changing.
  • ×Interiors that look the same in Tulum, Ubud and Ibiza.
  • ×Yoga schedules printed by the hotel, not built for your group.
  • ×Check-in desks, room cards, minibars, spa menus.
Common questions

What retreat leaders and private guests ask most.

The short answers. For anything else, write to Darina directly.

What makes a property a Nomadikas home? +

Three things, in this order. One — an architectural hook: rammed earth, an architect's name, a material you can't replicate elsewhere. Two — a week that changes people: the land has to do something, not just look like something. Three — the goosebump check: Darina arrives, breathes, and if the hairs on her arms don't stand up at the threshold, the property doesn't make the list.

Rejection rate is somewhere around ninety percent.

Is every home available for full buyout? +

Yes. Buyout is the default. For private weeks, the whole home is yours — no other bookings, no other guests on the land. For retreats and events, buyout is required.

A handful of homes accept individual room bookings during specific weeks. Ask us which — it changes by destination.

Can I host a wellness retreat at a Nomadikas home? +

Yes — this is what most of the portfolio is built for. Yoga, pilates, breathwork, menopause, sobriety, writing. Every home has either a dedicated shala or a convertible space; every home has chef capacity for retreat catering; every home has done at least one retreat before.

Do you handle corporate offsites? +

Yes — it's the fastest-growing part of Nomadikas. Groups of eight to twenty-six. Engineering leadership, C-suites, founding teams. We coordinate transfers, dietary needs, activity schedules, and — if you want — a facilitator.

How far ahead should I book? +

Private rentals: three to six months shoulder season, six to nine months peak (July–August in Europe, December–March in Costa Rica and Bali).

Retreats: nine to fourteen months — the group signup cycle runs longer.

Corporate buyouts: two to four months is typical, outside holiday windows.

What's included in the rental? +

Always: the full home, all rooms, housekeeping, welcome basket, arrival transfer. Usually: in-house chef for breakfast and dinner, daily coffee, basic yoga props. Optional: dedicated retreat host, teacher introduction, full-board catering, private excursions.

Every enquiry gets a line-by-line inclusions sheet before you commit.

Do you help plan the week, or just provide the home? +

Both — you choose. Some guests want the keys and a restaurant recommendation. Others want Darina to shape the arc of the week. The default sits in the middle: a complete welcome pack with everything we know about the land and the region. You decide how much of it you use.

What's the typical price range? +

Nightly rates run €1,800 to €12,000 depending on home, season and group size. Weekly retreat buyouts typically sit between €28,000 and €110,000 all-inclusive.

Every enquiry gets three home options at different price points with transparent breakdowns.
