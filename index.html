Common questions

What retreat leaders and private guests ask most.

The short answers. For anything else, write to Darina directly.

What makes a property a Nomadikas estate? +

Three things, in this order. One — an architectural hook we can point at: rammed earth, bamboo vaults, an architect's name, a material that isn't replicable elsewhere. Two — a week that changes people: the land has to do something, not just look like something. Three — the owner's standards: we only list estates where the operator cares as much as Darina does.

Most properties we look at don't make the list. The rejection rate is somewhere around nine in ten.

Is every estate available for full buyout? +

Yes. Buyout is the default way Nomadikas estates operate. For private weeks (families, friend groups), the whole property is yours — no other bookings, no other guests on the land. For retreats, buyout is required and includes full use of the shala, kitchen, staff, and transfers.

A handful of estates do accept individual room bookings during specific weeks of the year. Ask us which — it changes by destination.

Can I host a wellness retreat at a Nomadikas estate? +

Yes — this is what most of the portfolio is built for. Yoga, pilates, breathwork, menopause, sobriety, writing, grief, corporate offsites — all common. Every estate has either a dedicated shala or a convertible space; every estate has chef capacity for retreat-style catering; every estate has done at least one retreat before.

When you enquire, we match you to the estates that fit your group size, practice type, and budget — not the whole list.

Do you handle corporate offsites? +

Yes, and it's the fastest-growing part of what we do. Engineering leadership teams, C-suites, founding teams — groups of 8 to 26 taking a week somewhere that isn't a hotel. The estate gives you whiteboards that are fields, boardrooms that are shalas, and evenings that are dinners the team actually wants to be at.

We coordinate transfers, dietary requirements, activity schedules, and — if you want — a facilitator. Most groups book 6 to 10 weeks out.

How far ahead should I book? +

For private rentals: 3–6 months for shoulder season, 6–9 months for peak season (July/August in Europe, December–March in Costa Rica and Bali).

For retreats: 9–14 months. Retreat weeks book earlier because the group signup cycle is longer, and the best dates across all seven estates are gone by the March before.

For corporate buyouts: 2–4 months is typical and usually works outside of holiday windows.

What's included in the rental? +

Always: the full estate, all rooms, all common areas, housekeeping, welcome basket, arrival transfer from the nearest airport.

Usually: in-house chef for breakfast and dinner, daily coffee service, basic yoga props, pool/beach setup.

Optional: dedicated retreat host, teacher introduction, activity planning, full-board catering, private excursions.

Every enquiry gets a line-by-line inclusions sheet before you commit. No surprises.

Do you help plan the week, or just provide the estate? +

Both — you choose. Some guests want the keys and a recommendation for the best restaurant in town. Others want Darina to shape the arc of the week — sunrise activities, local partners, evenings built around who's coming.

The default is somewhere in the middle: we send a complete welcome pack with everything we know about the land and the region, and you decide how much of it you use.

What's the typical price range? +

Nightly rates across the portfolio run from €1,800 to €12,000 per night depending on estate, season, and group size. Weekly retreat buyouts (all-inclusive, staffed, catered) typically sit between €28,000 and €110,000 per week.

Every enquiry gets three estate options at different price points, with transparent breakdowns. Nothing gets quoted that isn't spelled out line by line.
