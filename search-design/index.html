Three audiences. One inventory.
Three ways to search.

Most travel sites force every visitor through one search box. Nomadikas serves three jobs-to-be-done — private renters, retreat guests, retreat hosts & event bookers — each with different first-decision axes. The homepage stays clean: three doors. The search lives where it pulls its weight, on the Homes and Venues landing pages.

01 · Homepage

Three doors, no search.

The brand moment. Hero film, three audience doors, retreats centered as the soul. Search is two clicks deep.

02 · Homes search

For private renters.

Where · When · Who. The Airbnb pattern reset for editorial restraint — Plum Guide minimalism, Le Collectionist warmth.

03 · Venues search

For hosts & events.

For what · When · Group size · Stay. A different first decision — event type — and a curator CTA next to the bar.

The thinking

What best-in-class search bars actually do — and which patterns Nomadikas borrows from.

Airbnb

The pill. Four-segment inline bar with a dark search button on the right. Sets the global expectation for "Where · When · Who". We borrow the structure, drop the chunkiness.

Plum Guide

Editorial restraint. Thin dividers, lowercase nav, generous white space, no aggressive promo language. We match the tone exactly — Nomadikas should feel like its older sister.

Le Collectionist

The luxury rental pattern: a quiet bar that lets the photography speak. Headline-led hero. We keep the photography priority — bar always overlaid on a real moment, never on a flat field.

Mr & Mrs Smith

Audience-first routing — different doors for different intents on the homepage, search lives one level down. This is exactly Nomadikas's homepage logic: three doors, then search.

Tagvenue / Peerspace

Event-search platforms that lead with event type as the first field, not location. The Venues page borrows this — "For" comes before "Where", because a wedding planner thinks event-first.

Aman / Six Senses

"Speak to a curator" CTA next to the search bar — high-touch luxury never lets the bar be the only path. Both prototype pages include this.

The simple version

A single unified search bar on the homepage is not the right answer.

Three audiences with three different first decisions can't share one bar without diluting all three. The homepage uses three doors. Each door leads to a landing page with a search bar tuned to that audience. Power users get a small persistent bar in the sticky header. That's the model.
